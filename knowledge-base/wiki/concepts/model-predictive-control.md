# Model Predictive Control

Model Predictive Control (MPC) is an advanced method of process control that iteratively computes locally optimal control signals by solving an optimization problem over a future time horizon. It explicitly handles constraints and relies on a predictive model to forecast future behavior.

## Key Points
- **Optimization:** The cost function $J$ to be minimized typically involves tracking error and control effort. In neuromodulation, objectives vary by pathology: desynchronization for Parkinson's, synchronization for epilepsy, or amplitude reduction for depression [[raw/Thesis.md#Cost function | Thesis]].
- **Predictive Models:**
    - **Linear Models:** Often used for simplicity (e.g., ARX or state-space models).
    - **Nonlinear Models:** Use [[concepts/volterra-model.md|Volterra Series]] or neural networks (e.g., DCNN).
    - **Population Dynamics:** While single-cell models like [[concepts/fitzhugh-nagumo-model.md|FitzHugh-Nagumo]] or [[concepts/hodgkin-huxley-model.md|Hodgkin-Huxley]] can be coupled to simulate populations, MPC often relies on reduced-order models like [[concepts/neural-mass-model.md|Neural Mass Models]] or Wilson-Cowan models for computational tractability [[raw/Thesis.md#Prediction model | Thesis]].
- **State and Parameter Estimation:** Since many neural states are not directly measurable, state observers (e.g., moving horizon estimators) are essential. Additionally, parameter estimation accounts for long-term effects like plasticity or disease progression [[raw/Thesis.md#Prediction model | Thesis]].
- **Constraint Handling:** MPC allows for hard constraints on stimulation intensity and rate of change, ensuring physiological safety and avoiding tissue damage [[raw/Thesis.md#Constraints | Thesis]].
- **Hierarchical Control:** To achieve millisecond sampling times required for real-time neural control, a hierarchical structure may be used where a fast low-level controller tracks a reference generated by a slower MPC [[raw/Thesis.md#Master's project | Thesis]].
- **LLM Integration:** Large Language Models can be integrated to automatically design treatment protocols, integrate multimodal feedback (EEG, patient/physician assessments), and propose stimulation parameters [[raw/Thesis.md#Master's project | Thesis]].
